I agree with Virginia Wolf when she said,"One cannot think well, love well, sleep well, if one has not dined well." Food plays a very essential role in helping us live comfortably and healthily. Not just any food does the trick,but Ghana jollof does it for me. Jollof rice with chicken and salad is one of my favourite foods.

Origin of meal

For some years now,there has been a Ghana-Naija war, concerning where jollof originated from and which tastes better. On November 4th,Google doodle celebrated 'Jollof Day' from which we larnt that jollof rice is an authentic West African dish prepared with tomatoes,onions,pepper and other local spices. The origins of jollof can be traced to the Senegambian region that was ruled by the Wolof Empire in the 14th century,spanning parts of today's Senegal, the Gambia and Mauritania where rice was grown.

Ingredients

Ingredient Measurement
Cooking oil 5-8 fl.oz
Onions 6 medium sized onions
Tomato paste 2 medium sized sachets
Pepper 15
Tomatoes 7medium sized
Garlic 2 cloves
Ginger 2 small root
Perfumed rice 500 grams
Salt as desired
powdered rose mary spice 2 tablespoons
Powdered nutmeg 1 small sachet
Maggi shrimp seasoning 3 cubes
Chicken thighs 6 pieces
Carrot 2 medium sized
Bell pepper 1 large sized
Lettuce Medium sized
Eggs 2
Heinz baked beans 1 small size
Mayonnaise 5 teaspoons
Sardine 1 tin
Avocado 1

Yam balls

Banku & Tilapia

Fried plantain with beans

Jollof Rice


Method of preparation

  1. Wash and chop into pieces 1 bulb of onion,1 ginger root 1 clove of garlic and blend with 3 pieces of pepper
  2. Thoroughly wash your chicken and add the blend to your chicken so it cooks
  3. Add a cup of water, a Maggi cube and a teaspoon of nutmeg to the chicken and allow to cook for 10-15 minutes
  4. Add salt to taste
  5. While the chicken is cooking, set a cooking pot on fire and pour some cooking coil as desired
  6. Chop thinly sliced onions into the cooking oil for flavour
  7. Add your tomato paste to the cooking oil and let it fry for some timw while constantly stirring
  8. Blend your tomatoes,pepper, 4 bulbs of onions, ginger garlic and pour the blend into cooking
  9. Allow the blend and the tomato paste to cook uniformly
  10. When the chicken is cooked up, drain its stock and fry the chicken in hot cooking oil
  11. Add the chicken stock to the stew for taste
  12. Take the chicken out of the oil when it is crispy and brown
  13. After the stew has cooked for sometime,add your powdered spices and 1 maggi cube
  14. Wash your rice and add to the stew
  15. Add just enough water to cook the rice and add another Maggi cube and salt to taste
  16. Add two eggs to the jollof for boiling
  17. While the jollof cooks, wash your lettuce, carrot, onion and chop them into preferred sizes
  18. Mix the vegetable together in a salad bowl and add a little brinewater to taste
  19. Pour your sardine oil on the vegetables and mix thoroughly
  20. Add your baked beans, sardine,sliced avocado and mayonaise to the vegetables
  21. Take the cooked eggs out of the jollof, remove its shell and slice them into the salad bowl
  22. Mix thoroughly and there you have your salad
  23. Put a clean napkin over the jollof when it has low moisture content so the rice and cook uniformly.
  24. Wash the bell pepper and chop it into the jollof for a nice aroma
  25. Serve your jollof when it is uniformly cooked with the salad and chicken
